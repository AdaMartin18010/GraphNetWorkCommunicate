## 1.1 Objects and Structures

- **Objects**: Quantum system $Q=(\mathcal{H}, \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{M})$, where $\mathcal{H}$ is the Hilbert space, $\mathcal{O}$ is the operation set, $\mathcal{M}$ is the measurement set
- **Structures**: Quantum bits, entangled states, quantum channels, quantum protocols, quantum keys, quantum networks

## 1.2 Basic Relations

- $R_{ent}$: Entanglement relation
- $R_{meas}$: Measurement relation
- $R_{chan}$: Channel relation
- $R_{prot}$: Protocol relation

## 1.3 Important Theorems and Reasoning

- **Theorem 1.3.1** (No-Cloning Theorem): Unknown quantum states cannot be perfectly copied
- **Theorem 1.3.2** (Bell's Inequality): Criterion for distinguishing classical correlation from quantum entanglement
- **Theorem 1.3.3** (Quantum Key Distribution Security): QKD protocols can generate secure keys even under eavesdropping
